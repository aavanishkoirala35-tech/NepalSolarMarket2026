Overview

Nepal Clean Energy Week 2026 brought together policy makers, developers, investors, technology providers and financial institutions to discuss Nepal’s clean energy future.


The conference highlighted opportunities, uncertainties, and the urgent need for realistic and strategic solar development.

“Nepal does not need more solar for the sake of numbers; it needs the right solar for the right places.”

Key Questions Raised

  • How should solar be developed in Nepal?
  • What role should solar play in the national grid?
  • Why is this not clearly reflected in policy?
  • What power should be prioritised for export?

Basic Discussions

Conversations were introductory and awareness-oriented.

Investors are still learning true solar economics.

Core Strategic Risk

Solar deployment is advancing faster than Nepal’s system integration capacity.


  • Grid instability
  • Higher long-term costs
  • Inefficient investments
  • Stranded assets

Conclusion & Way Forward

Nepal’s solar market is in a high-momentum, low-maturity phase.

“The future value lies in integration, not just generation.”

HPLN can create long-term impact by enabling integrated, compliant and sustainable energy systems.
