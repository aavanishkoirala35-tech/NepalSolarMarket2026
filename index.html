“Nepal does not need more solar for the sake of numbers; it needs the right solar for the right places.”

Market Insights

Emerging Market

Nepal’s solar ecosystem is still early-stage with growing awareness but limited technical maturity.

Hydropower Dominance

Solar should complement Nepal’s hydro-based renewable system instead of replacing it.

Grid Weakness

Weak transmission infrastructure and limited smart-grid capability remain key bottlenecks.

Policy Instability

Changing tariffs, licensing delays, and regulatory inconsistency create investor uncertainty.

Conference Observations

What Was Observed

  • Aggressive foreign supplier entry
  • Knowledge gaps in solar economics
  • “Solar = cheap energy” narrative
  • Grid uncertainty & approval reassessment

Key Questions Raised

Key Themes from Discussions

Deployment & Technology

Clear national vision, financing upgrades, policy predictability, and accountability.

Financing Focus

Strategic Recommendations for Nepal

Rooftop Solar

Encourage distributed rooftop systems rather than land-intensive utility projects.

Hybrid Hydro-Solar

Combine hydro and solar for seasonal balancing and resilient distributed systems.

Smart Grid Infrastructure

Upgrade transmission systems and integrate forecasting and grid intelligence.

Policy Reform

Improve transparency, licensing efficiency, and long-term energy planning frameworks.

Grid & System Architecture

400kV Transmission

  • National backbone infrastructure
  • Bulk power transfer
  • Cross-regional grid stability
  • Supports national-scale balancing

33kV Distribution

  • Localized distributed integration
  • Mini-grids & rural electrification
  • Industrial captive generation
  • Hybrid hydro-solar systems

Key Risks & Bottlenecks

Policy Risks

  • Changing tariffs
  • Licensing delays
  • Weak approval systems
  • Regulatory inconsistency

Infrastructure Risks

  • Weak transmission planning
  • Grid integration gaps
  • Technology dependence
  • Distribution bottlenecks

Environmental Risks

  • Land-use conflicts
  • Deforestation pressure
  • Ecological degradation
  • Unsustainable deployment

Nepal’s renewable future depends on smart integration, grid intelligence, sustainability, environmental protection, and long-term strategic planning.
